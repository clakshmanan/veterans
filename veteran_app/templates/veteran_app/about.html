Honoring the Past, Serving the Present, Securing the Future

The Indian Coast Guard Veteran Welfare Association (ICGVWA) stands as a beacon of hope and solidarity for the brave men and women who have dedicated their lives to safeguarding India's maritime frontiers.

Our Mission

To be the premier organization championing the welfare of Coast Guard veterans across India, fostering a strong community that celebrates their service while ensuring their continued well-being and prosperity in civilian life. To provide comprehensive welfare support, advocacy, and camaraderie to Indian Coast Guard veterans and their families, ensuring they receive the recognition, benefits, and care they deserve for their selfless service to the nation.

Our Vision

As the youngest branch of the Indian Armed Forces, the strength of ICG veterans remains comparatively limited. Unlike their counterparts from the Army, Navy, and Air Force—who are supported by established welfare mechanisms such as the Zilla Sainik Board, ICG veterans presently lack equivalent institutional support frameworks. The ICGVWA serves to bridge this gap by providing essential welfare assistance to the ICG veterans.

How ICGVWA Uniques

Extensive Grassroots Reach through its Regional, State and District level workforce – From urban centres to remote villages, making a real impact Frequent online meetings to keep veterans connected and confirm their wellbeing

Programs & Initiatives

The strength of ICG veterans is very less as compare to other armed forces being a youngest service and unlike veterans of the Army, Navy, and Air Force who have access to facilities like the Zilla Sainik Board (ZSB), ICG veterans currently lack similar dedicated support structures. The Indian Coast Guard Veteran Welfare Associatioin (ICGVWA) fills this gap by providing essential welfare and connectivity for retired ICG personnel.

Our Story

Born from the collective spirit of brotherhood and service, ICGVWA was founded by a group of dedicated Coast Guard veterans who recognized the need for a unified platform to address the unique challenges faced by their fellow veterans.

What began as informal gatherings among retired Coast Guard personnel has evolved into a structured organization spanning across all Indian states, providing essential services from pension assistance to healthcare support, career guidance to family welfare programs.
